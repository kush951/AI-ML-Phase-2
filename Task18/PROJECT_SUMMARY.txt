================================================================================
8. KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

Finding 1: Skill Overlap is Most Important (28.7%)
→ The ratio of required skills a student has verified is far more predictive
  than any other feature. Validates domain knowledge in hiring context.

Finding 2: Multi-Model Works Well (8.7% improvement)
→ Gradient Boosting beats other models meaningfully. Ensemble approach could
  provide additional robustness in production.

Finding 3: False Positives Acceptable (11.8% FPR)
→ Better to recommend and let humans decide than wrongly exclude candidates.
  Hiring context favors recall over precision.

Finding 4: Experience Matters but is Secondary (16.5%)
→ Students with strong skills can learn domain-specific techniques faster.
  Skill match + learning ability > rigid experience requirements.

Finding 5: College Context Matters (improves decision)
→ Same college students have better outcomes. System recognizes and factors this.
  Useful for placement officers making decisions.
